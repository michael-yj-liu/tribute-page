Desmond Thomas Doss

The man who single handedly saved dozens of lives in World War II

Here's a timeline of Desmond Doss' life

  • 1919 - Born in Lynchburg, Virginia.
  • 1942 - Enlisted for military service despite being offered a deferment because of his shipyard work.
  • 1942 - 1944 - When Desmond joined the Army, he assumed that his classification as a conscientious objector would not require him to carry a weapon. He wanted to be an Army combat medic. However, was assigned to an infantry rifle company. His refusal to carry a gun caused a lot of trouble among his fellow soldiers. They attempted to court martial him for refusing a direct orderâ€”to carry a gun. But they failed to find a way to toss him out, and he refused to leave. He believed his duty was to obey God and serve his country. But it had to be in that order.
  • May 5th 1945 - During the operation to capture Maeda Escarpment in Okinawa, Japan, also known as Hacksaw Ridge, a sudden vicious counterattack by the Japansese forced the American's to retreat. Desmond disobeyed those orders and saved the lives of 75 soldiers that day.
  • May, 1945 - A few days later as the Americans slowly took Hacksaw Ridge Desmond was severely wounded by a grenade and sniper bullet.
  • May 21st, 1945 - Desmond was evacuated from Japan aboard the USS Mercy
  • October 12th, 1945 - Desomond receives the Medal of Honor from then president, Harry S. Truman.
  • 2006 - After being hospitalized for difficulty of breathing Desmond died at his home in Piedmont, Alabama at the age of 87.
  • 2016 - The feature film, Hacksaw Ridge, based on Desmond's life is released.
